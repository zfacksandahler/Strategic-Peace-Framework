# Section 4: A Comprehensive Veterans' Health and Employment Bill

## Preamble

America owes a sacred debt to the men and women who have served in our armed forces. Too often, veterans return home to a system that fails to provide adequate health care, transition support, and meaningful employment opportunities. This section outlines a comprehensive, fully funded plan to honor our promise to veterans by addressing these gaps and ensuring they can thrive in civilian life.

## 1. Health Care Expansion and Modernization

### 1.1. Universal Access to VA Health Services
*   Extend eligibility for VA health care to **all veterans**, regardless of discharge status or length of service, with zero out‑of‑pocket costs for service‑connected conditions.

*   Double the number of VA mental health professionals and create a dedicated **Veterans’ Mental Health Corps** to provide outreach and counseling in rural and underserved areas.
*   Mandate evidence‑based, trauma‑informed treatment for PTSD, traumatic brain injury (TBI), and moral injury.

### 1.3. Women Veterans’ Health
*   Expand gender‑specific health services at every VA medical center and require annual cultural‑competency training for all VA staff.
*   Create a nationwide network of **Women Veterans’ Health Coordinators** to ensure equitable access and quality of care.

## 2. Seamless Transition to Civilian Life

### 2.1. “Boots‑to‑Careers” Transition Program
*   Provide every separating service member with a **personalized transition plan** developed at least 12 months before separation, covering education, career, housing, and health needs.
*   Expand the existing Transition Assistance Program (TAP) to include guaranteed interviews with federal contractors, apprenticeship placements, and small‑business startup grants.

### 2.2. Education and Skill‑Building
*   Make the Post‑9/11 GI Bill fully portable to family members and extend the use‑by deadline to **20 years after separation**.
*   Create a **Veterans’ Skills Accelerator Fund** that partners with unions, community colleges, and tech‑bootcamps to provide tuition‑free certification in high‑demand fields (e.g., renewable energy, cybersecurity, advanced manufacturing).

## 3. Employment and Economic Security

### 3.1. Federal Hiring and Contracting Preferences
*   Strengthen the existing veterans’ preference in federal hiring by requiring agencies to **fill at least 10% of new positions with veterans** annually.
*   Increase the set‑aside for service‑disabled veteran‑owned small businesses (SDVOSBs) in federal contracting from 3% to **5%**, with streamlined certification and bid‑support services.

### 3.2. Private‑Sector Partnerships
*   Establish a **Veterans’ Employment Tax Credit** for businesses that hire veterans and retain them for at least two years, with an enhanced credit for hiring veterans with service‑connected disabilities.
*   Launch a national “Hire‑a‑Veteran” campaign that highlights veteran‑friendly employers and provides free job‑matching platforms.

### 3.3. Entrepreneurship and Small‑Business Support
*   Triple the funding for the VA’s Office of Small & Disadvantaged Business Utilization and create a **Veterans’ Business Incubator Network** in every state.
*   Offer zero‑interest startup loans and grants to veteran‑owned businesses that locate in economically distressed communities.

## 4. Funding and Accountability

### 4.1. Peace Dividend Allocation
*   Dedicate **20% of the annual “peace dividend”**—savings achieved by reducing overseas military engagements and Pentagon waste—to fully fund the initiatives in this section.

### 4.2. Oversight and Transparency
*   Create an independent **Veterans’ Policy Board** composed of veterans, health‑care experts, and labor representatives to monitor implementation and recommend adjustments.
*   Require annual public reports on veteran employment, health outcomes, and program utilization, disaggregated by race, gender, and era of service.

## 5. Conclusion

Honoring our veterans is not just a moral imperative; it is a strategic investment in America’s human capital. By guaranteeing quality health care, smoothing the transition to civilian life, and ensuring economic opportunity, we can turn the “peace dividend” into tangible improvements in the lives of those who have served. This bill lays out a concrete, fully funded path to fulfill that promise.
